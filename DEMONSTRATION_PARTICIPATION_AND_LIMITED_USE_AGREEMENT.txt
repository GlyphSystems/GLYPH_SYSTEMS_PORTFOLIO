DEMONSTRATION PARTICIPATION & LIMITED USE AGREEMENT

Purpose

This agreement outlines the terms and expectations for participation in live technical demonstrations involving the Geometric / Spiral Logic computational framework and associated systems.

The purpose of these demonstrations is to allow approved observers, developers, researchers, or potential partners to evaluate live system behavior, telemetry, processing flow, and structured workloads in a controlled environment.

Participation in demonstrations constitutes acknowledgment and acceptance of the terms outlined below.

---

Scope of Demonstrations

Demonstrations may include:

- Live workload execution
- Runtime telemetry observation
- Processing comparisons
- Stability testing
- Controlled benchmark scenarios
- AI-related workflows
- Structured data processing
- Parallel execution observation
- Geometric execution visualization
- Observer-provided test workloads (subject to approval)

Demonstrations are intended for:

- Evaluation
- Observation
- Technical discussion
- Research
- Partnership consideration
- Educational review

Demonstrations are NOT intended as production deployment environments.

---

Observer Rights

Approved observers may be permitted to:

- Observe demonstrations live
- Record sessions
- Capture screenshots
- Review exported logs or summaries
- Submit approved workloads for testing

Recording permissions apply only to the live session itself and do not transfer ownership or licensing rights to proprietary systems or frameworks.

---

Proprietary System Protection

The following remain proprietary and protected:

- Internal system architecture
- Framework structure
- Execution methodology
- Protected implementation details
- Infrastructure configuration
- Internal tooling
- Proprietary computational methods
- Trade-secret operational processes

Participation in demonstrations does NOT grant:

- Ownership rights
- Replication rights
- Redistribution rights
- Reverse-engineering rights
- Commercial licensing rights
- Source-code access rights

No observer may claim ownership of the demonstrated framework or associated systems.

---

Reverse Engineering & Unauthorized Use

Observers agree not to:

- Attempt unauthorized replication
- Reverse engineer protected components
- Misrepresent the demonstrated systems
- Redistribute protected materials as their own
- Use demonstrations to create unauthorized derivative systems
- Misuse exported telemetry or session materials

Any commercial licensing, integration, or deployment discussions must occur through separate written agreements.

---

Demonstration Outputs & Session Materials

Logs, telemetry, exported session summaries, screenshots, and runtime observations are provided for evaluation and discussion purposes only unless otherwise agreed in writing.

Demonstration outputs do not constitute:

- Guarantees of production performance
- Guaranteed business outcomes
- Warranty of fitness for a specific purpose
- Legal certification
- Security certification
- Regulatory approval

Observers assume responsibility for how they interpret or apply demonstration results.

---

Safety & Legal Restrictions

Demonstrations may not be used for:

- Illegal activity
- Unauthorized intrusion testing
- Malware deployment
- Financial fraud
- Offensive cyber operations
- Real-world attacks or disruption
- Harmful automation
- Unauthorized surveillance
- Market manipulation
- Illegal data collection

The system operator reserves the right to reject any workload, request, or demonstration scenario for safety, compatibility, ethical, or legal reasons.

---

Limitation of Liability

Demonstrations are provided on an observational and evaluation basis.

The system operator shall not be held liable for:

- Business losses
- Financial losses
- Operational losses
- Misuse of outputs
- Misinterpretation of results
- Third-party implementation decisions
- Observer-provided workload behavior

Observers participate voluntarily and assume responsibility for their own evaluation decisions.

---

Session Approval & Participation

All demonstrations are subject to:

- Scheduling approval
- Scope review
- Compatibility review
- Legal compliance
- Safety review

The system operator reserves the right to terminate or deny demonstrations at any time.

---

Closing Statement

The purpose of the live demonstration process is to provide direct technical observation through measurable execution, runtime telemetry, and structured system behavior under controlled conditions.

The system is intended to be evaluated operationally through live observation rather than abstract description alone.
