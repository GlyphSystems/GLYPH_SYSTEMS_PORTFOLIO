EXTERNAL WORKLOAD & SUBMISSION EVALUATION AGREEMENT

Purpose

This agreement outlines the terms governing the temporary submission, evaluation, and controlled processing of external workloads, scripts, datasets, models, benchmarks, or related technical materials during live demonstrations involving the Geometric / Spiral Logic computational framework and associated systems.

The purpose of this process is to allow approved participants to observe how selected materials behave within a controlled demonstration environment.

This agreement is intended to clarify ownership, usage boundaries, evaluation scope, and legal responsibilities for both parties.

---

Ownership of Submitted Materials

All externally submitted materials remain the property of the submitting party.

This includes, but is not limited to:

- Source code
- Scripts
- Datasets
- Benchmarks
- AI models
- Processing workflows
- Structured files
- Technical documents
- Configuration files
- Related intellectual property

Submission of materials for demonstration or evaluation purposes does NOT transfer:

- Ownership rights
- Licensing rights
- Commercial rights
- Distribution rights
- Replication rights
- Patent rights
- Authorship claims

The system operator makes no ownership claim over externally submitted materials.

---

Limited Evaluation Scope

Submitted materials may be temporarily:

- Processed
- Executed
- Evaluated
- Observed
- Compared
- Routed through controlled demonstrations
- Included in runtime telemetry observation

Such usage is limited strictly to:

- Demonstration purposes
- Technical observation
- Evaluation sessions
- Research discussion
- Partnership review
- Structured testing scenarios

No submitted material shall be commercialized, redistributed, or intentionally disclosed outside the agreed demonstration scope unless separately authorized in writing by the submitting party.

---

Submitter Responsibilities

The submitting party represents and warrants that they:

- Have the legal right to submit the materials
- Possess authority to authorize testing or evaluation
- Are not knowingly submitting illegal, stolen, or unauthorized material
- Understand the demonstration environment is observational and evaluative in nature

The submitting party agrees not to provide:

- Malware
- Ransomware
- Unauthorized intrusion tools
- Illegal payloads
- Harmful automation
- Unauthorized surveillance tools
- Financial exploitation systems
- Real-world attack frameworks
- Illegal datasets or unlawfully obtained data

The system operator reserves the right to reject any submission at their sole discretion.

---

No Certification or Warranty

Evaluation or demonstration of submitted materials does NOT constitute:

- Security certification
- Production certification
- Regulatory approval
- Warranty of performance
- Warranty of compatibility
- Legal approval
- Financial endorsement
- Operational guarantee

Demonstration results are observational and informational only.

The submitting party remains solely responsible for:

- Production deployment decisions
- Security implementation
- Regulatory compliance
- Business usage
- Commercial deployment
- Third-party integration decisions

---

Confidentiality & Handling

Submitted materials may be temporarily retained for:

- Session preparation
- Technical evaluation
- Runtime observation
- Result generation
- Demonstration continuity

Reasonable efforts may be made to avoid unnecessary disclosure of submitted materials during demonstrations.

Upon written request, submitted materials may be deleted following completion of the agreed evaluation process unless retention is legally required or separately authorized.

---

Limitation of Liability

The system operator shall not be held liable for:

- Business losses
- Financial losses
- Operational interruptions
- Commercial outcomes
- Misinterpretation of results
- Third-party deployment decisions
- Security assumptions made by the submitting party
- Behavior of externally submitted workloads

Participation in demonstrations and submission of materials occurs voluntarily and at the submitting party’s discretion.

---

Demonstration Boundaries

Participation in workload demonstrations does NOT establish:

- A partnership
- Joint ownership
- Commercial licensing
- Investment agreement
- Employment relationship
- Automatic exclusivity
- Technology transfer rights

Any commercial agreements, integrations, licensing arrangements, or deployment rights must be negotiated separately in writing.

---

Session Approval & Review

All submissions remain subject to:

- Compatibility review
- Scope review
- Safety review
- Legal review
- Demonstration feasibility
- Resource availability

The system operator reserves the right to deny, pause, or terminate demonstrations involving external workloads at any time.

---

Closing Statement

The purpose of external workload demonstrations is to allow structured technical observation under controlled conditions while respecting the ownership, boundaries, and legal responsibilities of all participating parties.

All demonstrations are intended to support transparent technical evaluation without transferring ownership or proprietary rights between parties.
